# Design Context — Michael Lombardi Portfolio

> This file defines the design direction for Michael Lombardi's portfolio site. It guides every design skill (`/impeccable`, `/shape`, `/critique`, `/polish`, etc.) when making visual and interaction decisions. Tactical implementation rules (file paths, component structure, existing hex codes) live in `CLAUDE.md` at the project root — this file is concerned with *why*, not *what*.

## Design Context

### Users

Dual audience for a dual-purpose site. Michael is building a combined **professional + creative resume** for:

- **Hiring managers** evaluating him for Growth / Operator / SDR / AM / Head of GTM roles at scale-stage or enterprise companies
- **Startup founders** scouting co-founders, a Head of Growth, or a GTM lead with zero-to-one experience
- **Recruiters** sourcing for growth, sales, or founder-track placements

Their job-to-be-done when they land on this site: **verify both taste and substance** — does this person think, ship quality, and have opinions? AND does the track record (real outcomes, real numbers, real stories) hold up?

The site must serve both proofs in sequence. It cannot pick one. That's the core constraint.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **Confident. Disciplined. Tasteful.**

- **Confident** — The work speaks for itself. The site doesn't oversell. No "About Me" pitch paragraphs. No taglines straining to impress. No list of skills in the hero.
- **Disciplined** — Restraint is the design choice. Empty space is intentional. Every element earns its pixels. Nothing decorative.
- **Tasteful** — Every choice reads as deliberate — type, spacing, motion, color. The site reads like a well-set magazine feature, not a resume and not a template. Large typographic statements framed by silence.

**Emotional target on first impression**: "This person has taste" — before a single word of content has been read.
**Emotional target after scrolling the experience section**: "This person is serious."

### Aesthetic Direction

**Primary reference**: [stabondar.com](https://stabondar.com) — specifically its first viewport. What makes it feel slick isn't the effects; it's the discipline in spite of having those effects available. Pure black. One concrete visual moment. Everything else at 5–15% opacity. Negative space as composition. Nothing animating in the first frame.

**Anti-references / what this is NOT**:
- The current portfolio's own hero (as of April 2026) — scramble intro, parade of 7 cycling descriptor blocks, mouse-smoke canvas, scattered numbers at 28% opacity, outline name bleeding off the bottom at full stroke. Constant motion in the middle of the viewport. This is the exact "AI-template piled-on effects" look to move away from.
- Agency-template portfolios with card-grid case studies, dark-mode glassmorphism, neon gradient accents, sparkline decoration.
- Resume-first portfolios that lead with bullet points and years of experience.
- Overdesigned "animation showcase" portfolios where the animation is the content.

**Theme**: Dark. Pure black (`#000000`) background. Chosen because (a) the primary reference is dark, (b) the tonal direction is serious/operator-grade, and (c) it inherits from the existing site and is not up for reconsideration unless Michael explicitly wants to explore a light variant.

**Palette**: Monochromatic + one accent. Background `#000000` (or near-black tinted toward red). Single accent `#FF3A2D` (tomato red). Body text at 50–70% white. No secondary accent colors. No gradients on text, ever. Neutrals should tint subtly toward the accent hue (chroma 0.005–0.01) for subconscious cohesion.

**Typography**: General Sans is the primary typeface (already loaded in `fonts.css`). Filled solid red (`#FF3A2D`) for major statements — H1 role names, act numbers, company labels. Outline/stroke treatment is *only* for subliminal texture (background elements at ≤15% opacity). If a second typeface is introduced, it must not come from the Impeccable-banned font reflex list (Inter, DM Sans, Fraunces, Playfair, Cormorant, Newsreader, Lora, Plex, Outfit, Plus Jakarta, Instrument, Space Mono/Grotesk, Syne). Look further — consider distinctive display faces from Pangram Pangram, Klim, ABC Dinamo, or equivalents.

### Design Principles

**1. Restraint is the design.**
Empty space is a statement, not absence. When in doubt, remove. The first viewport should feel quiet, complete, and confident — not a parade of progressively-revealing animations. If a user has to wait 15 seconds for the hero to "finish," the hero is wrong.

**2. One focal moment per viewport.**
The eye should land on exactly ONE thing first. Not three, not five. Stabondar's opening composition has the car. Ours needs the equivalent — one deliberate visual anchor per screen, with everything else at subliminal weight.

**3. Design is the first proof. Substance is the second. Both are load-bearing.**
The hero earns the visit by being designed. The experience section earns the visit by being real — $2M raised, 40K+ fans, President's Club ×3, actual first-person stories. Neither half carries the whole alone. Don't sacrifice taste to cram in credentials. Don't sacrifice content for mood.

**4. Texture lives below perception.**
Decorative detail — coordinate numbers, outline type, ambient motion — operates at 5–15% opacity. Noticed peripherally, never read. If a visitor consciously notices the texture, it's too loud. The stabondar numbers are at ~10%. The current site has them at 28% — that gap is the problem.

**5. Motion serves the moment, not itself.**
Every motion beat must answer one question: *what does this help the user feel or understand?* Page entry is a moment. A hover revealing a descriptor is a moment. A section title sliding in on scroll is a moment. A constantly-cycling parade of descriptor blocks, mouse-smoke particles, and perpetual ambient shimmer are NOT moments — they're visual noise. Cut them.
